Balcony going uhhh swimmingly. Forget-me-not started flowering first, on the 4th of May.

![First forget-me-not flowers]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/forgetmenot.jpg)

Followed by an absolute explosion of lily pads all racing up to greet the water's surface around late May.

![First balcony lily pads]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/balcony_lily.jpg)

Much to my delight the plants seem to have ferried in some molluscan hitchhikers when they were delivered. I started to notice aquatic snails galore, with eggs being laid under the lily pads and among the hornwort.

![First balcony snails]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/balcony_snail.jpg)
![Snail eggs on the underside of a lily pad]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/balcony_snail_egg.jpg)

And then a few weeks later, I found a damselfly larva, great sign of biodiversity and that an ecosystem is establishing itself.

![Damselfly larva in the balcony pond]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/balcony_damsel.jpg)

Plus bonus pic of cat number 2.

![Cat chilling by my balcony pond]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/img/bonus_cat_2.jpg)

At this point my balcony experiment seemed to be pretty successful, so my attention started to turn towards grandiose ideas of POND EXPANSIONISM...
