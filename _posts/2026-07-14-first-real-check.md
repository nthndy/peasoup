Six weeks in and the water has finally settled. Ammonia and nitrite are both
reading zero, which means the little ecosystem is doing its job.

Phosphate is the one to watch. It has climbed to 0.25 mg/L, and the frogbit is
looking yellow around the edges. I suspect the two are linked, so I'll hold off
feeding anything and skim off the worst of the floating growth.

Everything else is either thriving or steady. The hornwort is romping away and
doing most of the oxygenating. Full numbers are on the [water page](/health/).
